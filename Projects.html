Our Featured Projects

Banaue Rice Terraces

Known as the "Eighth Wonder of the World," these ancient rice terraces were carved by the Ifugao people over 2,000 years ago. They are a testament to the ingenuity of early Filipinos in adapting to their mountainous environment for agriculture. The terraces provide stunning panoramic views, especially during the planting and harvesting seasons.

Mountain
Chocolate Hills Bohol

Over 1,200 dome-shaped hills that turn chocolate brown during the dry season, creating a stunning landscape. These natural formations are one of the most iconic landmarks in the Philippines. Tourists can visit the Chocolate Hills Complex or hike up the viewing deck in Carmen for a breathtaking sight.


Hill
El Nido (Palawan)

Famous for its towering limestone cliffs, turquoise waters, and stunning beaches like Nacpan Beach and Big Lagoon. El Nido is a paradise for island hopping, snorkeling, and kayaking. The Bacuit Archipelago, with its hidden lagoons and marine sanctuaries, offers an unforgettable experience.


Island
Coron

A world-class diving destination with sunken shipwrecks, Twin Lagoon, and the crystal-clear Kayangan Lake. Coron is known for its diverse marine life and vibrant coral reefs, making it a haven for scuba divers and nature lovers. The island also boasts breathtaking viewpoints and pristine beaches.

Diving Spot
Siargao

The "Surfing Capital of the Philippines," known for Cloud 9 waves, stunning beaches, and the serene Sugba Lagoon. Apart from surfing, visitors can explore rock pools, take island-hopping tours, or relax in its laid-back island vibe.



Island
Boracay

World-famous for its powdery White Beach, crystal-clear waters, and vibrant nightlife. This island is a top destination for water sports like parasailing, kite surfing, and scuba diving. Boracay also offers luxurious resorts, top-notch restaurants, and a fun-filled party scene.


Beach
Batanes

Features rolling green hills, traditional stone houses, and breathtaking coastal views, making it feel like a different world. The province's untouched beauty, peaceful ambiance, and warm Ivatan culture make it a must-visit destination.


Historical
Camiguin Island

Dubbed the "Island Born of Fire," it boasts volcanoes, hot springs, waterfalls, and the stunning White Island sandbar. Camiguin offers a perfect mix of adventure and relaxation, with unique sites like the Sunken Cemetery and the Ardent Hot Spring.

Island
Taal Volcano

A unique volcano within a lake, offering scenic views and a thrilling hiking experience. Taal is one of the most active volcanoes in the country, and visitors can trek to its crater to witness the stunning volcanic landscape.


Volcano
Hinatuan Enchanted River

A deep-blue, mystical river believed to be enchanted, famous for its crystal-clear waters. The river is known for its surreal beauty, and visitors can swim in its cool waters or watch the daily fish-feeding ritual.


River
Mayon Volcano

Known for its perfect cone shape, this iconic volcano is one of the most beautiful in the world. Mayon is also one of the most active volcanoes in the Philippines, and tourists can enjoy nearby attractions like Cagsawa Ruins and ATV adventures around its base.

Volcano
Sagada (Mountain Province)

A peaceful mountain town known for its hanging coffins, breathtaking caves, and sea of clouds. Adventurous travelers can explore Sumaguing Cave, trek to Marlboro Hills for sunrise, or experience the unique burial traditions of the locals.


Mountain
Tubbataha Reefs Natural Park (Sulu Sea)

A UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the best diving spots in the world, rich in marine biodiversity. The reefs host a variety of marine life, including sharks, manta rays, and sea turtles, making it a top-tier diving destination.


Beach
Apo Island (Negros Oriental)

A marine sanctuary famous for its vibrant coral reefs and friendly sea turtles. Visitors can snorkel alongside sea turtles and explore the stunning underwater world of this protected marine reserve.



Island
Vigan (Ilocos Sur)

A well-preserved Spanish colonial town with cobblestone streets and heritage houses, offering a glimpse into the past. Vigan's charm lies in its historical architecture, horse-drawn carriages (kalesa), and traditional crafts like pottery and weaving.
